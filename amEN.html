Introduction

Abu Obaida Al-Jarrah Preparatory School for Boys was established in 1988 and named after the great Islamic leader. It opened as a morning scientific school and graduated many students who excelled academically and morally. Its graduates contributed to serving society in various fields. The school was named after one of the great military leaders in the early days of Islam, the noble companion

Abu Obaida Amir bin Abdullah Al-Jarrah

One of the ten promised Paradise and one of the first to convert to Islam. The Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him and his family) called him "the trustee of this nation." He was a prominent military leader who commanded Islamic armies in the Levant and achieved great victories

He led the Islamic armies in the Levant, achieving significant victories, and had a major impact on the Battle of Yarmouk against the Romans in 636 CE. Abu Obaida died from the plague in 639 CE and was buried in the Jordan Valley
